Overview

 

Licensed contractors bring specialized knowledge and skills to your project and are required to follow safety standards. Illegal work can increase the risk of injury, or lead to property damage, fines, and/or increased insurance premiums. 

Before you hire a contractor, verify that they hold a licence to perform regulated work in BC.  

The contractor information that appears within our database includes: 

  • their licence number, status, and type;
  • any enforcement actions taken within our jurisdiction;
  • the number of non-compliances over 180 days; and

Technical Safety BC endeavors to keep this registry as up to date and accurate as possible. However, the registry may not include the most current information available. Technical Safety BC is not responsible for any errors contained within and will strive to correct any inaccuracies as soon as possible. If you discover any discrepancies, errors, or omissions, please email us. This registry includes enforcement action information from January 1, 2022, onwards only. Not all enforcement history with respect to a particular individual or company will necessarily appear in the search results. If you are interested in the enforcement history of a licensed contractor prior to January 1, 2022, you can contact us via email or visit our annual State of Safety reports (2023, 2022, 2021, 2020, 2019).

In accordance with s. 20(4) of the Safety Standards Act, information about an individual who dies or a corporation that is dissolved or struck off the register of companies may be removed from this registry.

In accordance with s. 36 of the Safety Standards General Regulation, a person may apply to remove the publication of their enforcement action from this registry if at least seven years have passed since the enforcement actions was taken, they have complied with all requirements imposed under the enforcement action, and they have had no further enforcement action taken against them within the seven-year period.

The information in this registry is disclosed in accordance with s. 20 and 21 of the Safety Standards Act and s. 33 of the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act. If you have questions about the collection, use or disclosure of this information, contact the Records, Information and Privacy Analyst at 1 866 566 7233.
